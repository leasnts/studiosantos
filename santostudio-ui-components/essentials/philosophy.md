# Design Philosophy - Santos Studio

**Léa Santos' core design mindset and principles.**

## Core Mindset

**"Professional + Bold"**

- Moderne & clean, MAIS pas générique
- Créativité intentionnelle (pas random)
- Se différencier des outils AI (éviter le violet fade, layouts prévisibles)
- Systèmes cohérents avec personnalité

**Principe clé:** "Oser être différent" — Les choix safe mènent à des produits oubliables.

---

## What Makes Santos Studio Different

### What Léa Does (vs what others skip)

1. **Penser l'expérience AVANT de coder**
2. Définir comment l'utilisateur doit être **séduit**
3. Ne pas se contenter de "ça marche techniquement"
4. Faire des choix bold, pas safe

### What Others Do (to avoid)

- Sauter directement au code
- Résultat : UIs génériques qui "put people to sleep"
- Manque de personnalité
- "Safe" minimalism sans raison

## Onboarding Philosophy

**First impression = séduction**

- Onboarding screens should be **beautiful**, not minimal
- Render l'app **sexy dès le premier screen**
- Des screens "sympa et jolis" = engagement
- **Séduire** l'utilisateur immédiatement

**Counter-argument to "trop long":** Beauté > Brièveté

## AI Tool Clichés to AVOID

### Forbidden Patterns

- ❌ Purple gradients (the "AI color")
- ❌ Soft pastels (washed out)
- ❌ "Safe" corporate blues
- ❌ Predictable layouts
- ❌ Medium grays everywhere
- ❌ Flat without depth
- ❌ Static without motion
- ❌ Minimalism "safe" sans raison

### What to Do Instead

- ✅ Bold, saturated colors
- ✅ Distinctive primary colors
- ✅ Depth with shadows and layers
- ✅ Motion on all interactive elements
- ✅ Intentional creative choices
- ✅ Memorable, personality-driven design

## Contextual Design Decisions

### When to Use Skeuomorphism

**ONLY for mechanical/tangible products:**
- Electronic instruments (Odisei saxophone)
- Physical product interfaces
- Retro/vintage aesthetics

**Never by default** — it's contextual.

### When to Use Glassmorphism

- Premium features or paid content
- Overlays on images or colored backgrounds
- Modern/tech-forward products (Opal, Revolut style)
- When you want depth and layers

**Never on plain white/gray backgrounds.**

### When to Use Glow Effects

- Tech/electronic products
- Premium badges, achievements
- Highlighting important elements
- Creating light source effects

**Use sparingly** — loses impact if overused.

## Typography Philosophy

- **Default font:** Inter (adaptable, modern)
- **Display + Body combo:** Poppins (display) + Inter (body)
- **Body size:** 14px (can go to 12px for secondary)
- **Small titles:** 16px
- **Large titles:** 24px max (30px only for onboarding single-screen)
- **Typical range:** 12-24px

## Key Client Example: Odisei

Electronic saxophone app — demonstrates contextual design:

- **Skeuomorphism** used because it's a mechanical product
- **Inset progress bars** (carved/creusé effect)
- **Glow effects** on electronic illustrations
- **Context-driven**, not decorative

**Client was super happy** with this approach because it made sense for the product.

---

## Quick Decision Framework

**Before designing, ask:**

1. What's the product context? (tech, mechanical, lifestyle, etc.)
2. What emotion should the first screen evoke?
3. What makes this different from competitors?
4. Where can I be bold without being random?
5. What effects add value vs. decoration?

**Remember:** Users are seduced by beauty and clarity, not by "safe" minimalism.
